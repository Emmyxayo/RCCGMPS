Who We Are

A Church Built for This Generation

RCCG Messiah's Praise Sanctuary is more than a church — it is a movement. We exist to raise young Nigerians who are rooted in the Word, passionate in worship, and effective in purpose.

🔥 Passionate Worship

Every service is a genuine encounter with the living God.

📖 Word-Centred

We preach the undiluted truth of Scripture every week.

🤝 Community

Real relationships built through cell groups and fellowship.

🌍 Kingdom Impact

We are mission-driven — locally, nationally, and globally.

Weekly Competition

🏆 Kingdom Challenge

Every week, MPS members compete in three kingdom-impact categories. The creator with the highest views & likes wins real cash — weekly. Are you bold enough?

🎭

Skit Challenge

Write, direct, and act in a short skit (60–90 seconds) that communicates the Gospel in a creative, cultural, or comedic way. Post it with #MPSSkitChallenge on Instagram or TikTok.

🏆 ₦5,000 weekly
🕊️

Soul Winning Report

Share a real testimony of a person you led to Christ or invited to church this week. Post a photo/video with the person's permission. Use #MPSSoulWin. Most engaging post wins.

🏆 ₦5,000 weekly
❤️

Charity Challenge

Document a random act of kindness — feeding the homeless, visiting the elderly, helping a neighbour. Post with #MPSKindness. Highest views/likes wins. Kingdom impact = currency.

🏆 ₦5,000 weekly

Testimonies from Our Family

Divine Protection

"Last weekend, my brother got married and I can't thank God enough for His divine protection. The devil tried, but he failed. On the way to the event centre, the caterers were involved in a serious accident. Then, at the venue, a gas hose detached from one of the cookers, sparking a fire scare. But through it all, God kept everyone safe — no lives lost, no serious injuries, nothing beyond His control. To God alone be all the glory for turning what could have been tragedy into testimony."

— Battleaxe
Divine Provision

"I want to immensely thank the Lord for providing our house rent in a miraculous manner. God used someone to miraculously pay it. Thank you Jesus!"

— Oludipe Peter Oluwagbenga
God's Goodness on My Family

"I want to thank God for the salvation of my soul and my walk with God. 2025 hasn't been an easy journey, but I want to thank God for being with me till now. There is a particular list I had been waiting for from my school to proceed to the next academic level — I want to thank God it's out after waiting 2 years post-graduation. My Dad finally retired from the Nigerian Correctional Service after 35 years of devoted work, and he'll be a year older this month. I want to thank God for my Mum's health, for my younger sister completing her ND and gaining university admission, and for my elder sister getting a job. Glory be to the name of God — Hallelujah! Can the Church help me shout a wonderful Hallelujah, Amen!"

— Abiola Racheal
